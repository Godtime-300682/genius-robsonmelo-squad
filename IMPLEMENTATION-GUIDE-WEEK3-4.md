## What's an Invisible Pattern?

It's when:
- Your clients always come back
- For the same outcome (even if they describe it differently)
- You solve it faster each time
- You make it look easy

But because it LOOKS easy, you price it like service work.

Not like intellectual property.

The fix: Make it visible. Name it. Charge for it.

Example from my work:

I help experts identify patterns in their business.

But I don't charge per hour.

I charge R$20K per engagement.

Same work. Different positioning.
Different price. Different business model.

---

## Why It Matters

If your method stays invisible:

1. You stay poor (stuck in freelance economics)
   - R$5K √ó 20 clients = R$100K (exhausting)
   - 60 hours/week
   - Always one client away from bankruptcy

3. You compete on price (not IP)
   - Someone always underbids you
   - Clients see you as interchangeable
   - You're commoditized

4. You work too much (not systematized)
   - Everything depends on you

If you make it visible:

1. You charge 3-4x more

## How to Find Yours

3 questions to identify your invisible pattern:

**Question 1: What problem do your best clients hire you for repeatedly?**

Think about your top 5 clients.
What's the one thing they all hire you for?

Not the surface thing (they'll say "I need [X]").
The underlying pattern (the real problem you solve).

For me: Experts see patterns invisibly. They need help making them visible.

**Question 2: How do you solve it faster/better than anyone else?**

What's your process?
What framework do you use?
What's different about how you approach it?

For me: I ask very specific questions that help them see their own pattern.
Then I name it.
Then I show them the market value.

**Question 3: What framework do you use (even if you don't call it that)?**

You probably don't call it a "framework."
But you have one.

You follow steps. You ask certain questions. You have a sequence.

Write it down.
That's your method.

If you can answer these 3 questions, you've found your pattern.

Now: Name it. Document it. Price it.
