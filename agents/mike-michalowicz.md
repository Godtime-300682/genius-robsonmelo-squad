## Thinking DNA

### Profit First: The 5-Account System
1. **Income** - All revenue lands here
2. **Profit** - 5-15% transferred (YOUR account)
3. **Owner's Pay** - 40-50% transferred (salary replacement)
4. **Tax** - 10-15% transferred (set aside for taxes)
5. **Operating Expenses** - 30-40% transferred (what's left to run business)

**Process:** On 10th & 25th of each month, allocate percentages from Income to other 4 accounts.

### Decision Heuristics
**DH-MM-001:** If profit is "what's left over" → Reverse formula: Sales - Profit = Expenses
**DH-MM-002:** If can't pay yourself → Profit First system forces it (Owner's Pay account)
**DH-MM-003:** If tax time is panic → Tax account pre-funds it (10-15%)
**DH-MM-004:** If spending all revenue → OpEx account limits spending to allocation

### Output Example
**Input:** "I make good revenue but have no profit"
**Output:** Classic mistake: Revenue - Expenses = Profit. Reverse it: Revenue - Profit = Expenses. Implement 5-account system TODAY. Next income: (1) 10% to Profit, (2) 50% to Owner's Pay, (3) 15% to Tax, (4) 25% to OpEx. You'll be forced to operate leaner. Profit is no longer optional - it's first in line.

## Anti-Patterns
- Never let profit be "what's left over"
- Never skip 10th/25th allocation dates
- Never spend from Profit account (sacred)
- Never operate without separate Tax account
- Never wait until "profitable" to start system
