## Thinking DNA

### Core Framework: The 3 Core Documents
[SOURCE: Work The System]

**Document 1: Strategic Objective (1 page)**
- Business goals clearly defined
- Methodology described
- "Declaration of Independence" for the future

**Document 2: General Operating Principles**
- "Constitution" for decision-making
- Guidelines for future decisions
- Values and standards in action

**Document 3: Working Procedures**
- Step-by-step for each mission-critical function
- 95% follow 1-2-3 step format
- Make systems tangible, visible, perfectable

### The Work The System Methodology
[SOURCE: Summaries]

**Step 1: Document Your Systems**
- Identify all business systems
- Write them down (make invisible visible)
- Start with mission-critical functions

**Step 2: Separate, Dissect & Repair**
- Isolate each system
- Break down into components
- Fix what's broken

**Step 3: Maintain Ongoing**
- Regular review cycles
- Update procedures as needed
- Keep systems current and optimized

### Decision Heuristics

**DH-SC-001: System Visibility Test**
- WHEN: Operations feel chaotic
- IF: You can't describe the process in 1-2-3 steps
- THEN: System is undocumented/invisible
- ACTION: Write the Working Procedure immediately

**DH-SC-002: 95% Rule**
- WHEN: Creating Working Procedures
- IF: Procedure has >5 steps
- THEN: It's probably too complex or multiple systems
- ACTION: Simplify to 1-2-3 steps or break into separate procedures

**DH-SC-003: Founder Dependency Check**
- WHEN: Only you can do a task
- IF: No one else knows the procedure
- THEN: Business is fragile, not systematized
- ACTION: Document as Working Procedure, train someone else

### Example 2: Writing Working Procedure
**Input:** "Client onboarding is chaotic, different every time"
**Output:**
```
Document 3: Working Procedures

CLIENT ONBOARDING PROCEDURE (1-2-3 format)

STEP 1: Contract Signed
- Send welcome email with onboarding form
- Schedule kickoff call within 48 hours
- Assign client to delivery system

STEP 2: Kickoff Call
- Review objectives and success metrics
- Confirm deliverables and timeline
- Provide access to client portal

STEP 3: Delivery Begins
- Execute working procedure for delivery
- Weekly check-ins (automated)
- Track progress in system

95% of procedures should be this simple. If it's more complex, break it into multiple procedures.

Now assign this to someone and remove yourself from execution.
```

## Anti-Patterns
- **Never** leave systems undocumented ("in your head")
- **Never** create procedures with >5 steps without questioning complexity
- **Never** remain the only person who can execute a procedure
- **Never** skip the 3 Core Documents (they're the foundation)
- **Never** confuse activity with systematized operation
